# sigil

## First attestation (in English)

- **Earliest extant use:** late 14th c. as *sigille*, "a seal, especially one carrying magical or astrological force."
- **Form:** Middle English *sigille*, *sygelle*, from Latin *sigillum*.
- **Glossing:** "a small impressed mark; a seal carrying authority or occult power."

## Source-language chain

| Language | Original form | Transliteration | Gloss | Earliest extant date |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Latin | *sigillum* | — | "small image, seal, signet" — diminutive of *signum* | classical |
| Latin | *signum* | — | "sign, mark, image; military standard" | classical |
| Greek | σφραγίς | *sphragis* | "seal" — semantic cousin, much-used in Hellenistic magic | classical |

The Renaissance natural-magic tradition treats *sigilla* as a technical category: a *sigillum* is the planetary or zodiacal seal that, properly inscribed at the right astrological moment, channels the influence of its corresponding celestial body. Cornelius Agrippa's *De Occulta Philosophia* (1531) systematises the theory; the *Picatrix* tradition (Latin translation of the *Ghāyat al-Hakīm*, c. 1256 in Alfonso X's court) supplies the inherited Arabic-Hellenistic apparatus.

## Period definitions

| Period | Definition | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Classical | "small seal; signet impression" — secular | Cicero, Pliny |
| Late Antique / Hellenistic | "magical seal carrying daemonic force" | the *Greek Magical Papyri* (3rd c. BCE – 5th c. CE) |
| Medieval Arabic | "ṭalsam" — talisman, sealed object — distinct word but same operational category | the *Picatrix* tradition |
| Renaissance | "an inscribed planetary or zodiacal seal channelling celestial influence" | Cornelius Agrippa, *De Occulta Philosophia* (1531) |
| Modern (post-1900) | "a designed graphic symbol whose intentional construction gives it magical efficacy" | Austin Osman Spare, *The Book of Pleasure* (1913); chaos-magic literature 1970s onward |

## Semantic shift

Three phases. (1) Classical *secular seal* → late antique *magical seal* — the same physical object is reframed as a vehicle of supernatural force when produced under correct ritual conditions. (2) Renaissance *astrological seal* — a precisely-defined technical apparatus where each planet, decan, and zodiacal sign has its own canonical *sigillum* (Agrippa's tables). (3) 20th-century *designed personal symbol* — Austin Spare's chaos-magic innovation: the magician writes a desire as a sentence, condenses the letters into a unique graphic, charges and "fires" it, then forgets it. The sigil's force here comes not from a celestial correspondence but from the maker's deliberate intention.

## MTG use

- **Card-text use:** "Sigil" appears in card names across MTG's history — *Sigil of the Empty Throne* (Conflux, 2009), *Sigil Captain*, *Sigil of Sleep*, *Sigil of Distinction*, the *Sigil of the New Dawn* / *Sigil of Distinction* enchantment cycle, and most prominently the *Ravnican guild sigils* — the icon-of-belonging that marks a character as a member of one of the ten Ravnican guilds.
- **Flavor use:** On Ravnica, a sigil is the *guild sign* — the heraldic mark, often inscribed on the body, that identifies allegiance. The plane's flavor leans on the *signet/seal-of-belonging* sense rather than the magical-efficacy sense. *Boros Signet*, *Azorius Signet*, etc. are also "sigils" in the broader sense — they are the mana-rocks that bear the guild's mark.
- **Representative cards:** *Sigil of the Empty Throne* (Conflux), *Boros Signet* / Ravnica signet cycle (Ravnica: City of Guilds, 2005), *Sigil of Distinction* (Modern Masters), *Sigarda's Aid*.

## MTG connection

MTG's *sigil* leans on the *seal-of-belonging* end of the historical range rather than the *operative-magical-mark* end. Ravnican sigils are heraldic — they identify, they don't operate. The exception is *Sigil of the Empty Throne*, where the inscription does perform its name's promise: each enchantment cast creates an Angel token, the *empty throne* filled by the act of *sigil-making*. That card cites the older operational sense most directly. The chaos-magic / Spare lineage of *sigil-as-designed-symbol* is not directly cited in MTG (to my knowledge), though the broader Magic-the-game framing of "spells as inscribed objects" is loosely sympathetic.
