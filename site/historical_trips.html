Historical trips

Etymology / biography / tradition cards. Each card is a research artifact tracing a magical term through its source-language chain and into MTG's vocabulary.

Etymology

conjure high

Bring something into being by speech — the verb that named medieval and early-modern ritual evocation, now applied to MTG's Alchemy mechanic for creating cards inside the game.

golem high

An artificial humanoid animated by ritual — Hebrew *gōlem* "shapeless mass", canonical in the Maharal of Prague legend; in MTG, a creature subtype indicating constructed, often animated artifact-bodies.

scry high

Look at the top N cards of your library and rearrange them — a keyword named after the early-modern divinatory practice of *scrying* into reflective surfaces.

sigil high

A sealing mark with magical force — Latin *sigillum* "small seal", elaborated through Renaissance natural magic and 20th-c. chaos magic; in MTG, the name of a card-cycle and an icon-of-belonging on Ravnica.

sorcery high

A spell that resolves only at sorcery speed — named after the medieval legal-pejorative term for divinatory and harmful magic, *sortilegium*, "drawing of lots."
