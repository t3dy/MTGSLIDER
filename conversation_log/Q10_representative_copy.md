# Q10 — 10 representative themes with esoteric copy

## Verbatim user input

> chose 10 of the most representative slides in terms of mtg popular themes and creature types and write some copy for them engaging the disciplines of mtg as exemplified by our three content creators but also my interests in esotericism as you can find in my database projects in the dev folder like claudiens

## How I interpreted it

Three constraints:
1. Pick 10 *representative* themes (popular tribes/themes, not deep cuts)
2. Voice draws from the three creators (Sierkovitz / Rhystic / LLU)
3. Voice ALSO folds in user's esotericism — checked `Claudiens/AFSTYLING.md` and `CLAUDE.md` to ground the tone (present-tense analysis, active voice, Latin/Greek terms italicised on first mention, hedging only where evidence is thin, no claim without a source)

Picked 10 themes covering all five colors and all three creator voices, with esotericism appearing at varying intensity (light for tribal primers, central for alchemy/books).

## What I built

[writing/10_representative_themes.md](../writing/10_representative_themes.md) — ~3000 words, ten ~250-word entries:
1. Wizards primer (Rhystic) — Renaissance *magus* tradition
2. Goblins primer (Sierkovitz) — *pharmakos* / scapegoat figure
3. Zombies primer (Rhystic) — *Tabula Smaragdina* ascent-and-return
4. Elves primer (Sierkovitz) — Sídhe / *aes sídhe* of Irish folklore
5. Vampires with lifelink (Sierkovitz) — alchemical *vivificatio*
6. Dragons with haste (Rhystic) — apocalyptic dragon iconography
7. Angels with vigilance (LLU) — Pseudo-Dionysian angelic hierarchy
8. Lab and alchemy (Rhystic + hermetic) — Khunrath, Maier, Fludd, Dürer
9. Books in Magic (Rhystic + bibliographic) — grimoire / archive / cipher tripartite
10. Graveyard engines (Sierkovitz + alchemy) — *nigredo* and *prima materia*

Closing section: three integration paths (paste into thesis slide, wire as new speaker-notes voice in `notes.py`, publish as companion essay).
